Problem Understanding (Addressing Judging Criteria)
Root Causes of Inefficiency in Experimental Biology
The core problem LabNavigator AI solves is the inherent inefficiency in biological experimentation due to:

High-dimensional parameter space: CRISPR experiments have dozens of variables (target sites, delivery methods, cell conditions, etc.) creating millions of possible combinations
Resource constraints: Labs can only run a limited number of experiments due to time and budget constraints
Knowledge silos: Researchers often lack visibility into similar experiments conducted elsewhere
Cognitive limitations: Human researchers struggle to analyze patterns across high-dimensional spaces
Severity of the Problem
This inefficiency translates to:

Extended development timelines: Cancer therapies typically take 7+ years to move from concept to clinic
Resource waste: 42% of lab consumables are used in experiments that don't contribute to final solutions
Missed opportunities: Promising therapeutic targets remain unexplored due to experimental bottlenecks
